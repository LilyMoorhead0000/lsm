Integrating Writing and Design Portfolio

Design Beginnings


Design Fuego

We used Visual Studio Code to develop a "Get to Know Me" website, practicing our coding skills along the way. We learned how to embed images and text into the site. Using GitHub, we published the website with its own domain to showcase our work.

Logo A/B/C

Using Adobe Logo Maker and Canva, we designed three logos that represented our personal identity. I also aimed to explore ideas for potential business logos during this project. In Adobe, we entered text and selected icons or styles that matched our vision. We were able to move elements around, adjust colors, and modify text to refine our designs. These were our initial three drafts.

UX Testing Logo Project

After drafting the three logos, we conducted a UX design study with three participants. I asked a mix of reflective, behavioral, and visceral questions to gather meaningful feedback. The insights we received helped us evaluate the effectiveness of our designs. Based on the participants' responses, I made significant changes to my logos—keeping elements they liked, such as color, font, and spacing, while removing or replacing those that didn’t resonate. These final three logos were the result of that iterative design process.

Cartoon Portrait

Working with layers was essential in creating this cartoon portrait. To replicate the colors from the original image, we used the eyedropper tool to sample accurate shades. After selecting a color, the pen tool helped outline the specific areas we wanted to fill. The process was prone to errors, and I ended up restarting several times due to small mistakes. However, when the eyedropper didn’t quite pick up the exact tone I needed, it was easy to manually adjust the color to better match the desired look.

Good Design

By examining a strong example of effective design, we were able to break it down and identify the key features that contribute to its success. Throughout this process, we explored how to use tools like arrange, frame, and text, gaining hands-on experience with each. We analyzed specific elements within the design, such as color schemes, balance, typography, and visual hierarchy, and connected them to the design principles we've studied in class, deepening our understanding of how these components work together to create a cohesive and impactful composition.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

As an aspiring writer entering the professional world, I’m increasingly aware of how rhetoric, technology, and ethics intersect in the creation and distribution of content. Writing today extends beyond the page. Digital media platforms demand not only compelling storytelling but also a thoughtful consideration of audience, medium, and impact. I'm particularly interested in how technology shapes rhetorical choices and ethical responsibilities, from algorithm-driven visibility to the authenticity of voice in digital spaces. Exploring digital media will allow me to expand my writing practice while engaging with the tools and ethical questions that define modern communication.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

In exploring visual design alongside my writing practice, I applied core design principles such as color theory, spatial balance, and visual hierarchy during the logo development process. I conducted UX testing to gather user feedback, which guided my revisions and helped ensure that the final designs were both functional and visually engaging. In another project, I created cartoon portraits using layering, color-matching, and shading techniques to enhance depth and cohesion. These hands-on experiences not only strengthened my eye for design but also deepened my understanding of how visual choices communicate meaning—an insight I’m excited to carry forward as I continue to bridge writing with digital media.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging with others in a workshop environment has been essential to my growth as both an emerging designer. Collaborative feedback sessions have allowed me to view my work through fresh perspectives, helping me refine my rhetorical choices and design decisions. In writing workshops, I’ve learned to give and receive critique thoughtfully, while in design critiques, I’ve practiced articulating the reasoning behind visual elements like color, layout, and user experience. These interactions have strengthened my ability to communicate across mediums and reminded me that creative work, whether written or visual, is most impactful when shaped through dialogue and iteration.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision played a central role in my design process across multiple projects, especially in the development of my logos. In response to feedback from UX testing, I refined my logo designs by preserving effective elements and reworking areas that lacked clarity or impact. I focused on improving contrast, alignment, and typography to create a more cohesive visual identity. These cycles of critique and revision deepened my understanding of design fundamentals like composition, consistency, and visual storytelling, ultimately strengthening my ability to adapt and improve across different media formats.

5. Learn to present your work.

Learning to present my work has been an extremely difficult, but rewarding process. Figuring out how to successfully upload a link off of GitHub was a difficult process. As I attempted to upload my work, an error continued to occur. It was not until I completely reworked the link that it was successful. This process prevented future user errors from happening. Overall, presenting my work in this class has been filled with valuable lessons.

Short Form


Two-page Spread

For the two-page spread, we used InDesign to attempt at recreating a previously designed magazine spread. This task challenged me to match fonts and design using an AI font tool to properly match the original font. Additionally, using tools to eliminate the original writing to replace with the new allowed me to get more comfortable with InDesign. I found this project to be challenging in the way that it had to be identical to the original design, therefore, no mistakes. Even the slightest difference in font or placement for this project could be deemed as unsatisfying or poorly done. I definitely focused on perfecting the placement and font size and type for this project the most.

Infographic

For this project, I created a one page inforgraphic on InDesign to help aid people in the career of journalism. Using textboxes and simple graphics, I provided several different areas in which one could advance in to become a profficient journalist. Using inviting colors, I attempted to make this infogrpahic as approachable as possible.

Advertisement

For this design, I focused heavily on my audience. Being that the ad is focussed on a perfume, I felt it was important to think about what the brand Glossier typically goes for in terms of aesthics. In 50-100 words, I attempted to reach a more sophisticated crowd. I used InDesign to make the colors, font, layout, and text of the ad as mature looking and sounding as possible.

Accessibile Design

For this design, I focused on making everything on the page as accessible and informative as possible. Using InDesign, I focused on implementing approachable colors, simple designs, and plain language. With large numbers to guide viewers through the page and an accessible link to the design, users everywhere should be able to follow along through my design regarding assessible design in the field of writing.

In developing each project, I carefully consider its context and the message I want to convey, whether professional or creative. Ethics play a key role in my work, particularly when it comes to accessibility in design. Every design centers on how the audience will interpret it and how, as a designer, I can make it both visually engaging and accessible to all.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

The process of reproducing a two-page magazine spread provides an opportunity to apply foundational design principles—balance, contrast, and alignment—in a practical context. This exercise deepens my understanding of the subtle visual elements that contribute to effective design, which I can then incorporate into my future projects.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Engaging with my peers in a workshop setting has given me incredibly valuable feedback that has significantly improved my designs. The collaborative environment has proven to be both supportive and highly effective in helping me grow as a designer.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Each project is an opportunity to refine and elevate my ideas. Whether I’m adjusting a layout in InDesign or fine-tuning the copy in an ad, I’m constantly revising to ensure every piece is as effective and polished as possible. The variety of formats and creative challenges continuously pushes me to grow and improve my design skills.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting my designs is an essential skill that I’m continually working to strengthen. It’s not only about showcasing my work, but also about communicating the ideas and reasoning behind it in a way that connects with others. Each presentation helps build my confidence and improves my ability to express my creative vision effectively.
